Privacy Policy

Last updated: 24 February 2026

Purpose of This Privacy Policy

This privacy policy explains how Hitchr Ltd ("we," "us," or "our") collects, uses, stores, and protects your personal information when you use the Hitchr mobile application (the "Application"). Our goal is to be transparent about our data practices and to help you understand your privacy rights.

This policy applies to all users of the Application. By using Hitchr, you acknowledge that you have read and understood this privacy policy. If you have questions or wish to exercise your rights, please refer to Section 13 (Contact Us).

1. Information We Collect

Information You Provide

When you create an account and use Hitchr, you may provide:

  • Account details – your name, email address, and profile photo (uploaded directly or imported from your Google or Apple account)
  • Profile information – your date of birth, home location, a short biography, and your hitchhiking experience level
  • Adventure content – photos you upload when logging a hitch or adventure
  • Privacy policy acknowledgement – a record that you have accepted this policy, stored as part of your account

Information Collected Automatically

  • Location data – precise GPS location (latitude, longitude, altitude, speed, heading, and accuracy) during active hitchhiking sessions. See Section 4 for full details.
  • Hitchhiking statistics – total distance travelled, number of hitches, and countries visited, calculated from your activity
  • Device information – device model, operating system, and app version, used to diagnose technical issues
  • Usage and interaction data – how you use the app (features used, screens visited, session events), collected via PostHog and Firebase Analytics
  • Crash and error reports – technical crash data collected automatically via Firebase Crashlytics

Information from Third-Party Sign-In

If you sign in with Google or Apple, we receive your name, email address, and profile photo from those providers. We do not receive your passwords. Apple may share your name only on your first sign-in.

3. Information Sharing

We do not sell your personal data. We share your information only in the following circumstances:

  • With other Hitchr users – your profile (name, photo, bio, experience level) is visible to other users. Your real-time location is shared with other users only while Live Hitching is active and you have explicitly enabled sharing.
  • With service providers – third-party services that help us operate the Application (see Section 9). These providers are bound by data processing agreements and do not use your data for their own purposes.
  • For legal reasons – where required by law, court order, or government authority, or where we believe in good faith that disclosure is necessary to protect the rights, safety, or property of Hitchr, our users, or the public.
  • With event organisers and leaderboards – if you participate in a Hitchr-facilitated hitchhiking event, you may share your Hitchr user ID with the event's administration team, which grants them access to your route map for the duration of the event. No personal data (such as your name or email) is transferred to event organisers. Some events feature public leaderboards on which your name, team, and route progress may be visible; this will be clearly communicated before and during your participation.
  • With your explicit consent – when you specifically authorise us to share your information.

4. Location Data

Location is central to how Hitchr works. Here is exactly how we handle it:

  • During an active hitch – when you start a hitchhiking session, we collect your precise GPS location continuously. This includes latitude, longitude, speed, heading, altitude, and accuracy. This data is used to record your route and, if you enable Live Hitching, to share your position with nearby users in real time.
  • Live Hitching – your real-time location is written to Firebase Realtime Database and is visible to other Hitchr users while the feature is active. You can toggle sharing on or off at any time during a session.
  • Background location – the Application requests access to your location while running in the background so that your route is recorded accurately if you switch apps. You can revoke this permission at any time in your device settings, though doing so will prevent complete route tracking.
  • Stored route data – completed route data is stored in Firestore as part of your hitch records, allowing you to view your past routes and statistics.
  • Mapbox – we use the Mapbox SDK for map display and location search. Mapbox may process location and map interaction data in accordance with their own privacy policy.

You can control all location permissions through your device's settings at any time.

5. Photos and Media

When you upload photos to an adventure or set a profile picture, those images are uploaded to and stored in Firebase Storage. Profile photos may be visible to other users. Adventure photos are associated with your hitch records.

We request access to your photo library only when you choose to upload an image. We do not access your photos at any other time. When you delete an image or your account, associated images are removed from storage.

6. How We Store Your Data

Your data is stored using Firebase services operated by Google LLC, including:

  • Firebase Authentication – your user ID, email, display name, profile photo URL, and sign-in method
  • Cloud Firestore – your profile information, hitch records, hitchhiking spot data, and tracking sessions
  • Firebase Realtime Database – temporary real-time location updates during active sessions
  • Firebase Storage – your uploaded profile photo and adventure photos

Some data is also stored locally on your device (e.g. session state for offline resilience) using your device's local storage. This local data is cleared when you log out or uninstall the Application.

7. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your personal information:

  • Data in transit is encrypted using TLS; data at rest is encrypted within Firebase infrastructure
  • Access to your data is restricted to authorised personnel who need it to operate and improve the Application
  • Firebase services operate within Google's enterprise-grade security infrastructure
  • We review and update our security practices as the Application develops

No security system can prevent all potential breaches. If you believe your account has been compromised, please contact us immediately at hello@hitchr.world.

8. Your Rights

Under UK GDPR and other applicable data protection laws, you have the following rights:

  • Right to Access – request a copy of the personal information we hold about you
  • Right to Rectification – correct inaccurate or incomplete personal information (you can update most profile data directly in the app)
  • Right to Erasure – request deletion of your account and associated data
  • Right to Restrict Processing – request that we limit how we use your personal information
  • Right to Data Portability – request a copy of your data in a machine-readable format
  • Right to Object – object to processing based on legitimate interests, including analytics
  • Right to Withdraw Consent – withdraw consent at any time where processing is based on consent (e.g. Live Hitching, background location)
  • Right to Lodge a Complaint – file a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) in the UK, or your local data protection authority

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at hello@hitchr.world. We will respond within 30 days.

Additional controls available directly in the app:

  • Edit your profile and bio at any time in app settings
  • Toggle Live Hitching location sharing on or off during any session
  • Revoke location permissions through your device settings
  • Stop all data collection by uninstalling the Application

9. Third-Party Services

The Application uses the following third-party services, each with its own privacy policy:

  • Google Firebase – Authentication, Firestore, Storage, Realtime Database, Analytics, Crashlytics, Remote Config, and Cloud Functions
  • Google Play Services
  • PostHog – product analytics; receives your user ID, name, email, and app interaction events
  • Mapbox – map display and location search
  • Google Sign-In – if you choose to sign in with Google
  • Apple Sign-In – if you choose to sign in with Apple

We ensure all third-party integrations comply with applicable data protection regulations and only share the data necessary for the provision of those services.

10. Analytics and Tracking

We use analytics services to understand how users interact with the Application and to improve it over time:

  • PostHog – tracks app events (such as starting a hitch, using the map, and completing an adventure) and associates them with your user ID, name, and email. PostHog is configured with auto-capture disabled; only explicitly defined events are sent.
  • Firebase Analytics – aggregated usage metrics to understand feature adoption and app performance.
  • Firebase Crashlytics – automatically captures crash reports and error logs to help us fix bugs.

You can opt out of analytics data collection by contacting us at hello@hitchr.world.

11. Children's Privacy

Hitchr is not directed at children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you are a parent or guardian and believe your child has provided us with personal information, please contact us at hello@hitchr.world and we will delete it promptly.

12. Data Retention

We retain your personal information for as long as necessary to provide our services and comply with legal obligations:

  • Account and profile data – retained while your account is active. Upon account deletion, your data is removed within 30 days, except where retention is required by law.
  • Location and route data – retained as part of your hitch records for as long as your account is active. Deleted with your account upon request.
  • Real-time location data – temporary data in Firebase Realtime Database is cleared at the end of each session.
  • Photos – retained until you delete them or your account is deleted.
  • Crash and error data – retained for up to 90 days within Firebase Crashlytics.
  • Analytics data – retained for up to 24 months in identifiable form. After this period, data may be retained in aggregated, anonymised form indefinitely for long-term service improvement.

You may request earlier deletion of your data by contacting us at hello@hitchr.world. Some data may need to be retained longer to comply with legal obligations or to resolve ongoing disputes.
