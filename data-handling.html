How We Handle Your Data

A plain-English explanation of what we collect, what we share, and what you're in control of — especially during Hitchr events.

Our Privacy Policy covers the regulatory detail. This page is for everyone who wants a straight answer to the question: what actually happens to my data when I use Hitchr?

During Normal App Use

When you use the Hitchr app day-to-day, the data we collect is there to make the app work. That means your profile, your hitch routes, your stats, and your location during active sessions. We store it in Firebase (Google's infrastructure), we use it to power the app, and we use anonymised usage data to improve it.

We don't sell it. We don't share it with advertisers. The full picture is in our Privacy Policy.

During Hitchr-Facilitated Events

When you take part in a Hitchr-facilitated event — a race, challenge, or organised hitchhiking trip — a few things change. Here's what you should know.

What data is involved

You use your own Hitchr account on your own device, exactly as you would normally. There are no separate tracking devices or third-party apps involved — your route and location data are the same data your Hitchr account always holds.

The only data relevant to the event's running is your route map — specifically the location data produced during the event window.

What event organisers can access

Hitchr does not transfer your personal data to external event organisations. Instead, access works like this: you share your Hitchr user ID directly with the event team yourself, which gives them the ability to view your route map for the duration of the event. Nothing else — no name, email, profile, or account details — is passed to them.

Event organisers can only see location data produced within the event's time window. Your broader account history and profile remain entirely private.

What we can't fully control

Because event organisers can view your live route during an event, we can't control what they do with what they see — for example, they could take a screenshot or note your location. We require event partners to handle this access responsibly and only use it for the purposes of running the event safely, but we can't audit their internal practices.

If you have concerns about how a specific event partner used your route data, please contact us and we'll follow it up with them.

Public leaderboards

Some events feature public leaderboards. If yours does, your name, team, and progress data may be visible to anyone — not just other participants. This will be clearly flagged when you register for the event. If you don't want to appear on a public leaderboard, contact us or the event organiser before the event starts.

Your Tracking Data and Insurance

Since all tracking during events happens through the Hitchr app, your route and location data is stored in our own systems — specifically in Firebase Firestore and Firebase Realtime Database, which Hitchr Ltd controls.

This means that if there were ever an insurance or legal situation where accurate location records were required, Hitchr Ltd is able to retrieve that data from our systems. You do not need a separate tracking device or third-party app for this purpose.

We would only access or share this data in response to a legitimate insurance claim, legal request, or safety incident, and only to the extent necessary. We would notify you where we're legally permitted to do so.

Deleting Your Event Data

We don't automatically delete your data after an event ends — it remains part of your Hitchr account, which means your routes and stats stay in your history as normal.

If you want your event data (or any of your data) deleted, you can request this at any time by emailing hello@hitchr.world. We'll action your request within 30 days.

Because Hitchr doesn't transfer your personal data to event organisers, you don't need to chase third parties to get your data removed — your data lives in your Hitchr account, and deleting it there is enough.

A Note on Third-Party Event Partners

We've deliberately designed the event data model so that external organisations never receive a copy of your personal information. Our approach means you as a user / event participant remain in control of who can see your location, and that access is scoped strictly to the event window.

The limitation we do acknowledge is that we can't audit what an event team does with what they see on screen. We take that seriously and require responsible use as a condition of running events with us. If you have concerns about a specific event, please get in touch.
